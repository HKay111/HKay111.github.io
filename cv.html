Research Experience

Undergraduate Thesis: Carbon Emissions in Africa: EKC and Regional Integration Prof. Deepika Goel | Defended | Presented at UGREE 2026
This thesis examines two interconnected questions using a panel of nine African countries (Algeria, Kenya, Mauritius, Morocco, Mozambique, Namibia, Nigeria, South Africa, and Zambia) from 2006 to 2022.

Main Paper: Investigates the impact of regional integration on carbon emissions through regional value chains, environmental co-operation, and institutional integration. Using the Fully Modified OLS estimator, the study finds co-integration relationships and shows that trade openness and regional value chains increase consumption-based emissions, while environmental co-operation and institutional integration reduce them. Government effectiveness also significantly reduces emissions.

Side Paper: Examines the Environmental Kuznets Curve (EKC) across the same countries. Contrary to the conventional inverted-U EKC hypothesis, findings indicate a U-shaped relationship — carbon emissions per capita first decline with rising income, then increase beyond a certain threshold. Energy use per capita shows approximately one-to-one elasticity with carbon emissions per capita.
